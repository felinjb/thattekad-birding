A Legacy of Conservation

"The richest bird habitat in peninsular India, comparable only to the eastern Himalayas." – Dr. Salim Ali

The Discovery

In the 1930s, the world-renowned ornithologist Dr. Salim Ali conducted his famous bird surveys of Travancore and Cochin. When he arrived at Thattekad, a lowland forest nestled between the branches of the Periyar River, he was astounded by the sheer density and diversity of avian life.

The Sanctuary Today

Established in 1983 as the first bird sanctuary in Kerala, the Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary at Thattekad spans just 25 square kilometers, yet it harbors over 300 species of birds. Its unique mix of tropical evergreen, deciduous, and riverine ecosystems creates the perfect microclimate for rare endemics.

Our Star Residents

Birders travel from across the globe to our forests specifically to spot the elusive Ceylon Frogmouth, the vibrantly colored Malabar Trogon, the majestic Great Hornbill, and the Sri Lanka Bay Owl. Our indigenous guides have spent their lives learning the calls, nesting habits, and seasonal movements of these magnificent creatures.
