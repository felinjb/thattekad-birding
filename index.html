Welcome to the Jungle

The Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary at Thattekad is one of the richest bird habitats in peninsular India. Characterized by dense tropical evergreen and deciduous forests, it is a haven for both migratory and endemic bird species.

Our indigenous guides have spent their lives navigating these trails. Whether you are a professional photographer looking for the perfect golden-hour shot, or a nature lover wanting to see the legendary Ceylon Frogmouth, we tailor our treks to your exact needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

The ideal birding season in Thattekad is between October and March. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the sanctuary becomes home to a massive influx of migratory species alongside our resident endemics.

Yes, guests are required to bring their own optical and photography gear. While our guides are experts at spotting and positioning you for the perfect shot, we do not provide camera equipment or binoculars for rent at this time.

We offer tours for all fitness levels. Our "Village & Culture Walk" is very flat and relaxed, while the "Guided Sanctuary Trail" involves deeper forest trekking. If you have mobility concerns, please let us know when booking so we can customize a flat-terrain route for you.

During the dry season (Dec-March), leeches are very rare. However, if you visit right after the monsoon, they can be present. We highly recommend wearing closed-toe trekking shoes and long trousers. We also provide organic anti-leech remedies (like salt/tobacco powder) before entering the deep trails.
