The Jewel of the Forest: Spotting the Malabar Trogon

Published: April 30, 2026 | By Local Guide

The **Malabar Trogon** is widely considered one of the most beautiful birds in the Western Ghats. With its brilliant crimson underparts (in males) and orange-brown hues (in females), seeing one in the wild is a dream for many birders.

Where to Look

Unlike many birds that stay high in the canopy, Trogons often sit motionless on mid-level branches. They are "perch-and-pounce" hunters, meaning they sit still for long periods waiting for insects. This makes them excellent subjects for photography—if you can find them.

Photography Tips

Because they live in the deep, dark understory of Thattekad, light is often a challenge. We recommend a fast lens and a steady hand. Since they are sensitive to movement, we use a slow-approach technique to get you within lens range without disturbing them.
