Monsoon Herping Expedition in Thattekad – A Rainforest Adventure

When the monsoon clouds roll over the Western Ghats, the forests of Thattekad transform into one of the most exciting wildlife destinations in India. Hidden beneath wet leaves and along forest streams, a hidden world emerges.

Species Spotlight

During the rains, the amphibians of the Western Ghats take center stage. Here are two of the most sought-after subjects for macro photographers:

The Endemic Bronze Frog

The Iconic Malabar Gliding Frog

Why Thattekad is Perfect for Herping

While famous for birdwatching, Thattekad is also a premier location for herping in South India. The southwest monsoon (June to September) creates ideal conditions to spot specialized species like the **Malabar Pit Viper** and various arboreal frogs.

"The forests surrounding Thattekad are part of a UNESCO biodiversity hotspot, offering sightings of species found nowhere else on Earth."

Ethical Observation

At Thattekad Birding, we practice strict ethical wildlife observation. We ensure that our guests can photograph these incredible creatures without causing them any stress or habitat disturbance.
